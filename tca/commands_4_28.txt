Task: Update the classify_narrative() function in classify_tester.py 
to fix the scoring formula. Do not change anything else in the file.

Current problem: Tags with small case populations (e.g. 1-2 cases) 
are scoring higher than ground truth tags with large populations 
(e.g. 50 cases) because 100% frequency_pct on small populations 
inflates the score unfairly.

Two fixes to apply:

Fix 1 — Normalize by match count:
Change the profile_score from a raw sum to a mean:
  profile_score = sum(narrative_similarity * frequency_pct) / match_count
where match_count = len(overlapping_ids)
This makes scores comparable across tags regardless of indicator 
match volume.

Fix 2 — Apply population confidence weight:
After computing the normalized score, apply a confidence multiplier 
based on the tag's total_cases from rules_hypothesis:
  confidence = min(total_cases / 50, 1.0)
  profile_score = normalized_score * confidence

Where total_cases is retrieved from 
profile['total_cases'] in rules_hypothesis.

The final profile_score stored in the results should be this 
confidence-weighted normalized score.

Also update the printed output to show:
- The confidence weight applied
- The total_cases for that tag profile
alongside the existing tag, profile_score, and match_count fields.

Do not change classify_narrative() function signature.
Do not change how signals are loaded or how results are printed 
beyond the additions above.
